Minecraft Modifications

April 20, 2020

Overview

I am a Minecraft player, who has logged many hours on the game. I recently discovered resource packs and datapacks, and I wanted to encorperate them into my gameplay. After doing some research and looking at others, I created four of my own resource packs and one datapack. I have all five files avalible for download, including my custom JSON files. Enjoy!

Resource Packs

In the downloads found here, you can find four resource packs. One is the custom texture pack, one is an X-ray pack, and the other two are redstone customizations. The main pack has changes to some plants for easier identification of growth stages, as well as changes to the textures of many redstone components for ease of use. This includes on and off specific textures on pistons and levers, as well as arrows for hoppers, observers, and dispensers/droppers. The goal of this is to let for easier redstone and technical gaming while still keeping the basic feel of vanilla Minecraft.

The other two resource packs are soley for redstone wires. One displays the wires as bars stretching across the blocks, as commenly used by many (Codecrafted, Faithful, etc.). The other is less visually appealing, with the signal strength on the wire. There are two seperate packs for the reason of toggling between the two packs.

Datapack

My custom datapack includes custom crafting recipies to return blocks (such as half slabs, doors, fencegates, etc.) to their original material. It also has increased drop loot from both shulkers and wither skeletons. The goal of this is again to keep the vanilla feel of Minecraft while allowing for recipies that some players may find convienient. The drop rate increase is designed for servers where resources are limited.
